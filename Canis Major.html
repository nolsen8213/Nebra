Canis Major, The Great Dog

Canis Major is one of Orion’s hunting dogs, running through the winter night sky. It doesn’t look much like a dog, but it’s easy to find. Imagine a line through Orion’s belt. Follow it and you’ll come to Sirius, the brightest star in Canis Major and the brightest star in the night sky. Look carefully at Sirius. Does it twinkle, or sparkle with different colors?

The Inuit of the eastern Arctic call the star Singuuriq. That means “flickering.” To them, Sirius is the flame of an old woman’s seal oil lamp, flickering in the breeze from her window.

Inuit who live in the western Arctic call the star Kajuqtuq Tiriganniarlu. That means “red fox and white fox.: When they look at Sirius, they see two foxes fighting to get into the same hole. The star changes color because one fox is white and one is red.
