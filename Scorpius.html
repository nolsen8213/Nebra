Scorpius, The Scorpion

To the Greeks, a long line of stars form the giant scorpion named Scorpius. But to the Maori of New Zealand, the stars are a magic fish hook.

The fish hook belonged to a young boy named Maui. One day, Maui hid in his brothers’ fishing canoe. When the brothers found Maui, they were very angry. They let him stay, but they wouldn’t give him a hook or bait.

So, Maui took out a magic jawbone and used it as a hook. The young boy soon caught a huge fish and pulled it into the canoe. Suddenly the fish turned to stone, fell back into the ocean, and became islands. Maui tossed the magic hook into the sky. And, it’s still there for you to see- in the stars of Scorpius.
